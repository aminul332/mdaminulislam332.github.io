TAAWWUL

Concept: “Transition from Old to New”

Project Description

This project proposes the interior renovation of the KUET Central Mosque, focusing on creating a spiritually engaging and visually continuous prayer environment. The design blends Mughal-inspired elements with modern minimalism.

Light, shadow, and geometric patterns are used to shape the space. Perforated partitions replace solid walls, allowing visual flow while maintaining zoning. The integration of Islamic geometry and warm wood materials reflects cultural identity.

Key Features

  • Mughal-inspired minimal interior
  • Light & shadow driven space
  • Perforated visual continuity
  • Islamic geometry & calligraphy
